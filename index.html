Research Experience

My current research interests lie in Theoretical Computer Science, in particular counting complexity and harness. I have also done research in sustainability and economics.

MIT Theory of Computation

I worked in the MIT Hardness Group (Professor Eirk Demaine) within Theory of Computation (ToC) in MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Lab (CSAIL).

Counting Complexity of Solo Chess

June 2025 - Present

I read a lot of papers on counting complexity and applied them to the toy problem of Solo Chess. I proved ASP-completeness using reductions from hamiltonian paths and Kiki Euler tours. I also showed that those reductions could be generalized to some fairy pieces. Manuscript is currently in progress.

Hardness of Graph Threading in Grid Graphs

February 2025 - May 2025

I worked on the Graph Threading problem, which is the problem of threading a junction of degree n at least n-1 times. I looked at the case for grid graphs. In my attempt to position-relative NP-hardness, I found a polynomial time algorithm for a special case.

Mobile Balancing Problem

January 2025

My supervisor proposed the problem of balancing weights. I scoped the problem, defined cases. I found that it reduced to a variant of the matching problem which allowed ties.
